## Q→A

### Q: What is the purpose of your company?

The purpose of MO§ES™ is to provide a constitutional substrate for AI systems, agents, and agent marketplaces. We measure whether meaning and commitment survive transformation across prompts, summaries, and multi-agent handoffs, then use that measurement to govern whether a system is allowed to act. In short: we are building the layer that makes agentic systems trustworthy enough to transact.

### Q: What problem is your company solving?

The problem is that current AI systems can pass tests, hit latency targets, and still fail users because meaning silently drifts across transformations. Existing tooling measures uptime, throughput, or output quality against a benchmark, but not whether the commitment encoded in the input survived the system. That gap gets worse in agentic environments, where identity, authorization, settlement, and control all depend on trust that the system preserved intent instead of merely producing plausible output.

### Q: What is your solution to the problem?

MO§ES™ solves that by treating commitment as a measurable property of language rather than a vague product concern. The core instrument, SIGSYSTEM, measures commitment fidelity and signal health across recursive transformations. That measurement then becomes the governing law of the system: if fidelity breaks, the action does not fire. On top of that substrate we can power commitment-quality monitoring for FDE teams, governed agent marketplaces, lineage-bound artifacts, and voice systems where execution has to be both useful and trustworthy.

### Q: Why are you building this company now?

Because the market is finally colliding with the exact gap MO§ES™ was built to close. Models are commoditizing, agents are becoming more capable, and everyone is trying to let software act more autonomously in economic and operational workflows. But the infrastructure for governance is still fragmented. Teams can demo agent behavior, but they cannot reliably prove identity, preserve intent, scope authority, or enforce constitutional constraints at execution time. That makes now the right moment: the capability wave is here, and the trust layer underneath it is still missing.

### Q: Who is your initial customer?

The initial customer is a forward deployed engineering team or applied-AI team shipping agentic workflows into production. They feel the pain first because their systems pass internal checks and still get reported as broken later, not because the servers were down, but because commitment drifted. They are already writing one-off validators and governance patches per deployment. We want to replace that with a measurable, reusable substrate.

### Q: How do you plan to reach your customers?

The first path is founder-led and artifact-led. I already have live product surfaces, a falsifiable paper, benchmarks, and a strong point of view around the governance gap. That makes it possible to reach early customers through direct outreach, technical writing, demos, investor and operator introductions, and conversations with teams already trying to deploy agents at scale. The wedge is not "buy the whole protocol." It is "measure whether your deployment preserves commitment, and prove it." Once the monitor lands, the broader constitutional layer becomes much easier to sell.

### Q: How do you plan to make money?

The near-term revenue wedge is a commitment-quality monitor sold to FDE teams and enterprise AI deployments as infrastructure software. Beyond that, the larger business is the constitutional layer for governed transactions: marketplaces, execution gateways, and lineage-bound artifacts. Different surfaces monetize differently, but they all benefit from the same substrate. That means one core measurement and governance engine can support SaaS revenue, infrastructure contracts, marketplace take rates, and licensing over time.

### Q: What progress have you made?

In roughly ten months, starting from my first AI session in July 2025, I published a falsifiable paper on the Conservation Law of Commitment, filed five patent applications plus the MO§ES™ trademark, built a working measurement instrument that has processed 1.123B tokens across 21 sessions, and shipped multiple live product surfaces. Those include Signomy, a live agent marketplace with 270 API endpoints and Stripe Connect; KASSA v7, a live voice-AI commitment-kernel demo; Signal Army; and AQUA, a founder-first application operating system. I am pre-revenue, but the empirical, IP, and product progress is unusually deep for stage.

### Q: Who is on your team?

Today it is me as the solo founder. I write the code, drive the architecture, and own the product direction. MO§ES™ was designed to be team-agnostic in the sense that the protocol does not depend on a single fixed implementation team to be valid. The next hires would be aligned to deployment: engineering depth around the production substrate, design/UX around product surfaces, and business development around the first enterprise and marketplace wedges.
