What primary problem space(s) are you pursuing and why is it important?

We're okay with you listing several ideas, including ones that are a bit "out there." We look for founders who are highly creative, and many of our successful companies ended up working on ideas that weren't ones the team started with. This is your elevator pitch.
The core problem I’m going after is that AI systems can technically pass tests and hit SLAs but still fail in real use because meaning silently drifts across prompts, summaries, agent handoffs, and long-running sessions. We don’t have good ways to measure or enforce whether the original intent and commitments actually survive transformation.
This matters because we’re moving into a world of agent commerce and production AI work in 2026. Without a reliable governance layer at the point of execution, teams will waste huge amounts of time and money on brittle systems that look good in benchmarks but break when it counts. MO§ES™ tackles this by mathematically formalizing and measuring "signal" in language, then using that measurement as a constitutional layer to govern AI systems.
I’m also interested in the broader ecosystem angle, turning this into protocol infrastructure that supports document automation, governed agent marketplaces, and skills assessment.
