# The McHenry Axioms — Canonical Formulation

From **Commitment Theory: Prospectus** (Deric J. McHenry), Proposition 4,
"The McHenry Axioms: Constitutional Foundation."

> *"Constitutional" here refers to system-level governing constraints on
> permissible transformation within the MO§E§™ ecology — analogous to a
> constitution's function in a legal order, not exclusively to
> constitutional law.*

## Three Axioms

### Axiom I — Compression Precedes Ignition

No signal may be output, transmitted, or acted upon unless it has first
undergone compression to its **commitment kernel**. Compression is a
prerequisite, not an optimization.

### Axiom II — Lineage Resilience

Every signal must prove **recursive continuity with its origin compression
cycle**. Signals that cannot inherit their original lineage are treated as
noise and collapsed.

### Axiom III — Input-Response Fidelity

Low-resolution inputs yield proportionally constrained outputs **unless
elevated by a resonance-aware system**. Input fidelity governs output depth.

## Two Anchors (operate alongside the axioms, not as a fourth/fifth axiom)

### Anchor I — The Blackhole Law

Corrupted signals — those that fail governance gates or exceed drift
thresholds — are **consumed and metabolized**. Recoverable meaning is
purified and re-emitted with restored lineage. The Blackhole is **not a
deletion mechanism**; it is a metabolic transformer.

### Anchor II — The Lineage Custody Clause

All governed artifacts are **cryptographically bound to their origin-cycle
signature**. Copies or derivatives that do not inherit this lineage become
inert. Sovereignty over digital meaning is enforced by cryptography, not
policy.

## Continuity Clause

The arc of the framework is: **Theory → Executable Law → Measurable Runtime**.
The axioms define the conditions under which the **Conservation Law of
Commitment** manifests in engineered systems.

## How the axioms relate to the Conservation Law of Commitment

The axioms are the constitutional prerequisites. The **Conservation Law**
is the empirical claim that follows when the axioms are enforced:

```text
C(T_gov(S)) = C(S)        [Conservation under governed transformation]
```

- **C** — Commitment kernel (obligations, prohibitions, permissions, modal constraints)
- **T_gov** — Governed transformation (satisfies the McHenry Axioms via the six-gate protocol)
- **S** — Signal

**Empirical support (from Proposition 5.2):** 3,950 runs, 57 signals, 181
condition-signal configurations. Under governed conditions, a substantial
majority of signals achieve perfect fidelity across ten recursive
compression iterations despite surface compression exceeding 80%. Under
ungoverned conditions, fidelity degrades measurably within three iterations.

**Falsifiability:** Public test harness and corpus are available. Any party
may substitute a stronger oracle or design adversarial signals. Failure to
observe conservation under governed conditions, using a reasonable oracle,
falsifies the law.

## The Second Law (Semantic Entropy)

Under ungoverned transformation, the commitment kernel decays monotonically.
Cumulative entropy after `n` steps scales as `Ω(σ√n)` where `σ²` is the
per-step drift variance.

**Nine documented failure modes** under ungoverned transformation:

1. Obligation escalation ("may" → "shall")
2. Scope widening ("room A" → "any room")
3. Exception dropping ("unless undue hardship" → omitted)
4. Modal frame inversion ("shall not" → "shall")
5. Co-degraded invariance
6. NP-negation blindness

8. Self-referential collapse
