## Section 3 — Your POV

### Q9. What does agent-first look like for your business in 12 months?

Agent-first for us means governance moves from prompt etiquette to execution law. In 12 months, every meaningful handoff between humans, models, and agents in the MO§ES™ stack should be measured for commitment fidelity before it is allowed to act. That turns "did the workflow run?" into the higher-order question that matters: "did meaning survive?"

The business expression is concrete: a live commitment-quality monitor for forward deployed engineering teams, a governed agent marketplace where every transaction clears through constitutional checks, and product surfaces like AQUA and KASSA using the same substrate in production. Most teams today measure volume, latency, or accuracy. We are building the missing row-three category: deontic telemetry for whether the commitments encoded in the input still hold in the output. That is what agent-first looks like to me: not more autonomous demos, but agents that can be trusted because governance is built into the protocol layer itself.

## Section 4 — Your work

### Q10. What have you built with agents in the last 6 months?

In the last 10 months, starting from my first AI session in July 2025, I built the constitutional substrate and multiple live product surfaces on top of it. The core measurement instrument, SIGSYSTEM v1.1, has processed 1.123B tokens across 21 sessions and 7,327 turns. On top of that I shipped Signomy, a live agent marketplace with 270 API endpoints and Stripe Connect; KASSA v7, a live voice-AI commitment-kernel demo; Signal Army v1.4, which processed 76,313 words across 6 production runs; and AQUA, a founder-first application operating system. I also published a falsifiable paper on the Conservation Law of Commitment and filed five patent applications plus the MO§ES™ trademark. The common thread is that I am not just using agents to prototype. I am building the governance layer that determines whether agentic systems can be trusted in production.

### Q11. Walk us through what you're showing

In the first attachment, I am showing Signomy. This is the marketplace product surface and the clearest example of the agent-first business environment I am building. It is live, includes 270 API endpoints, uses Stripe Connect, and shows how governed agents and transactions can become a real product surface rather than just an internal prototype. If you start there, you are looking at the most concrete business expression of the system: agents, workflows, and transaction infrastructure living in one product environment.

In the second attachment, I am showing the benchmarks page at `mos2es.com/benchmarks`. This is the build and operating proof behind the system: 1.123B tokens processed, 21 sessions, 35,242 lines of code shipped across five build days, 94.66% cache hit rate, and #1 across all five measured kernels in the SIGSYSTEM benchmark set. I want this attachment to make one thing clear: the system is not just producing ideas, it is producing output under real production conditions with measurable performance characteristics.

In the third attachment, I am showing KASSA, the live voice-AI commitment-kernel demo. This is where the same core ideas get applied to voice. The point is to show that the system is not limited to one narrow interface or use case. The same measurement and governance layer can shape real-time voice behavior too, and in this case it shows up as a product surface where execution quality matters in real time. The clearest proof point there is efficiency: conventional NLU re-inference per call is around 50 seconds, while KASSA operating on the commitment kernel brings that to roughly 6.5 seconds.

In the fourth attachment, I am showing the Grok demo. This is a public-facing proof surface with 339 timestamped exchanges across 13 days, and it helps show that the ideas are not limited to one interface or one product format. It is useful because it gives a different kind of visibility than Signomy or KASSA: instead of marketplace or voice, it shows continuity of the system in a conversational environment over time. One highlight from that surface is that it makes the operating philosophy legible in public. It shows how the same system that drives the benchmarks and product work can also sustain long-form reasoning, iteration, and explanation in an open conversational setting.

Finally, I am attaching the paper. That closes the loop. The marketplace, benchmarks, voice demo, and public conversational proof are all downstream expressions of the same core model, and the paper is the cleanest way to show the formal foundation underneath them.

### Q12. Why you're the right person to build this

I am the right person to build this because I came at the problem from the measurement gap itself, not from inherited AI orthodoxy. I am a Rust Belt operator from Buffalo with 20 years of professional experience, and my first AI session was only in July 2025. In the ten months since, I went from zero AI credentials to a published falsifiable physics paper, five patent filings, a registered trademark, a working measurement instrument, and four live product surfaces.

What matters most is not just that I built it, but how I built it: under the same recursive stress the framework is designed to measure. That gave me an operator-level understanding of where agents actually fail in the wild. The result is not another wrapper or observability layer. It is a constitutional substrate for agentic systems built from first principles and already exercised in production-like work.
