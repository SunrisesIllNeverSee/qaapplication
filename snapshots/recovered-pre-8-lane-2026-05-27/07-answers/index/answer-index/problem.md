## QU-0030 · AP-cyberfund · 2026-05-23
The initial problem I set out to solve was building the first Operator-Based AI Leaderboard. Think video-game-style metrics, stats, and insights, except for AI users. Today, AI measurement is model-based only: benchmarks in self-contained tests, or users clicking like/dislike. There's nothing measuring what the human operator is actually doing inside their sessions.

That rabbit hole, which I love (as an avid gamer Halo/WoT Blitz), led me to Commitment Theory. To produce stats on users inside private conversations, you first have to identify what's being measured, and in AI there's only one substrate rich enough to carry that signal: language itself. Commitment Theory quantifies the unquantifiable.

From there, the bigger problem revealed itself: the same measurement instrument that scores operators also governs systems and agents, because they're language-based too. Quantified commitment lets us enforce behavioral constraints, sequence enforcement, and conflict resolution between agents and systems at the execution layer. Not before policies, and not after audits, instead at the moment the action fires. If the action doesn't meet the requirements, it doesn't fire.

## QU-0032 · AP-cyberfund · 2026-05-23
The increasing gap between the pace of technology's growth and the effects it has on our global society, moreover individuals. Everyone in AI is arguing about alignment, risk, or capability. Almost no one is naming this specific gap: between how fast intelligence is evolving and how fast we can anchor the future against it.

This is also a gamechanger the likes of which no one has seen before. The framing that matters is a united front between Biological and Artificial Intelligence — accelerated co-evolution, not rivalry, not replacement. That united front is what anchors the future to something worth arriving at. The gates are down, everyone now has access to information, intelligences, and tools unlike ever before — it is so exciting.
