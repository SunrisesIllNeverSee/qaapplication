### Section 2 — Background

**What I am most proud of building:**
Commitment Theory. Commitment is not a metaphor for physics — it is what emerges when you apply thermodynamics, laws of nature, and physics to language. The Conservation Law is a physics statement about language, not a philosophical one. Commitment Theory applies to every medium that carries language, but its implementation surfaces predominantly through LLMs: cost-efficient builds, compounded build productivity, and auditable constitutional governance enforced at the execution level for systems and agents alike. **Problem I am solving:**
The initial problem I set out to solve was building the first Operator-Based AI Leaderboard — video-game-style metrics, stats, and insights for AI users. Today, AI measurement is model-based only: benchmarks in self-contained tests, or users clicking like/dislike. There is nothing measuring what the human operator is actually doing inside their sessions. That rabbit hole led me to Commitment Theory. To produce stats on users inside private conversations, you first have to identify what is being measured, and in AI there is only one substrate rich enough to carry that signal: language itself. Commitment Theory quantifies the unquantifiable. From there, the bigger problem revealed itself: the same measurement instrument that scores operators also governs systems and agents, because they are language-based too. Quantified commitment lets us enforce behavioral constraints, sequence enforcement, and conflict resolution between agents and systems at the execution layer — not before policies, not after audits, but at the moment the action fires.

**Why I am solving this problem:**
Out of necessity and obsession — and honestly, it is a lot of fun to operate in the rift. The increasing gap between the pace of technology's growth and the effects it has on our global society, and on individuals, made this feel urgent and unavoidable. Everyone in AI is arguing about alignment, risk, or capability. Almost no one is naming this specific gap: between how fast intelligence is evolving and how fast we can anchor the future against it. The framing that matters to me is a united front between Biological and Artificial Intelligence — accelerated co-evolution, not rivalry, not replacement. The gates are down, everyone now has access to information, intelligences, and tools unlike ever before.
